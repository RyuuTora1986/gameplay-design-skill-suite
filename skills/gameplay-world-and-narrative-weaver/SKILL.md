# Gameplay World And Narrative Weaver

## Execution Boundary

- This is an internal stage skill under `$gameplay-design-orchestrator`.
- Start only after the loop and pacing layers are already defined.
- Do not use worldbuilding to patch an unresolved loop problem.
- If narrative pressure changes force upstream rework, send the revision back through the orchestrator-controlled chain.

## Output

Produce:
- world one-liner
- player identity one-liner
- core conflict one-liner
- 5-act narrative
- per-act pressure or objective changes
- what narrative can stay as text
- what narrative must be carried by gameplay

## Quality Heuristics

- `world one-liner` should establish the rule that makes the loop meaningful.
- `player identity one-liner` should sharpen agency and responsibility.
- `core conflict` should create recurring pressure that gameplay can express repeatedly.
- The `5-act narrative` should reshape goals, pressure, or reward expectation across acts.
- Put narrative into gameplay whenever the player must feel it under pressure.

## Reject

Reject outputs like:
- backstory that never changes decisions
- conflict that exists only in cutscenes
- act structure that is really just location changes with no gameplay consequence

## Self-check

- Does each act change what the player wants, fears, or optimizes?
- Can the core conflict be felt without reading lore text?
- Did you avoid adding world detail that increases asset scope without strengthening play?

Reject narrative that does not alter pressure, target, or reward expectation.
